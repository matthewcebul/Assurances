Ourlandia and Theirlandia have long contested control of Wave Island.
According to Theirlandia:

  • Theirlandia's ancestors originally discovered Wave Island nearly 200 years ago, settling there before Ourlandia's ancestors first arrived;
  • Theirlandia has maintained strong cultural ties with the island's inhabitants to this day;
  • Theirlandia therefore considers Wave Island to be an essential part of its national heritage.

    • According to Ourlandia:
  • Ourlandia's ancestors began searching for mineral reserves on Wave and Sun Islands over 200 years ago. While no minerals were found on Wave Island, diamond mining on Sun Island has developed into a highly profitable industry;
  • Sun and Wave Islands symbolize Ourlandia's proud history of naval exploration;
  • Ourlandia therefore considers Sun and Wave Islands to be essential parts of its national heritage.

    • Theirlandia began the current dispute by demanding that Ourlandia give up control of Wave Island to Theirlandia, threatening to take the island by force if necessary. Theirlandia expresses regret that Ourlandia has ignored Theirlandia's repeated statements of its sovereign rights over Wave Island, and its prior requests that Ourlandia abandon its claim to the island. Theirlandia asserts that it now has no choice but to demand control of Wave Island.

    Theirlandia further asserts that it does not desire any unnecessary conflict with Ourlandia and that control over Wave Island is the only source of controversy preventing harmonious relations between the two states; Theirlandia looks forward to a mutually beneficial peace once this issue is resolved.

    Several years ago, Theirlandia had a similar territorial dispute with another state, "Otherlandia." Otherlandia made concessions, and the two states have enjoyed peaceful relations since then.

    Military experts believe that, in the event of war, the balance of military power between Ourlandia and Theirlandia does not favor either side.
